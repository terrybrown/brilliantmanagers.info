Chapter 2 · Self
Understanding yourself as a manager
Before you can lead a team effectively, you need a clear-eyed view of how you operate — your instincts, your defaults under pressure, your genuine strengths, and the blind spots you've probably stopped noticing.
This isn't a personality test. It's an invitation to sit with a set of questions that most managers never get asked directly — and to answer them honestly.

💡 This chapter works best as a conversation. Use it with your own manager, a trusted peer, or a coach — not just as private reflection.
